Sometimes, late at night, when the world goes quiet and the stars keep their vigil outside the window — I let myself imagine.

I imagine laughter that never grows old. Mornings that taste like coffee and your name. Long drives that go nowhere and everywhere at once.

I imagine futures that feel like home — not a place, but a warmth. A presence. A person who makes ordinary moments glow like something sacred.

The truth is, the most beautiful tomorrows I've ever pictured always had you somewhere in them — a quiet constant in every dream I dare to keep.

So here is chapter four: the future I secretly imagine, offered to you — one soft breath at a time.
